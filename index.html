Overview

Tourette Syndrome is one type of Tic Disorder. Tics are involuntary, repetitive
movements and vocalizations. They are the defining feature of a group of childhood-onset, neurodevelopmental
conditions known collectively as Tic Disorders and individually as Tourette Syndrome, Chronic Tic Disorder (Motor or
Vocal Type), and Provisional Tic Disorder. The three Tic Disorders are distinguished by the types of tics
present (motor, vocal/ phonic, or both) and by the length of time that the tics have been present.
